# Copy Formulas & Story Scripts

## Headline Formulas

```
[Number] Ways to [Desired Result] Without [Pain Point]
How [Specific Person] Got [Specific Result] in [Timeframe] (And How You Can Too)
The [Adjective] Guide to [Topic] That [Authority] Don't Want You to See
Warning: Don't [Action] Until You Read This
"[Exact Quote From Customer About Result]"
```

## CTA Text (Never Use "Submit")

```
Good: "Get My Free [Asset]" / "Start My Trial" / "Show Me How" / "Yes, Send It!"
Great: "Get Instant Access" / "Claim My Spot" / "Start [Desired Result] Now"
Best: "Send Me The [Specific Asset Name]" / "Reserve My Seat (X Spots Left)"
```

## Suby's Power Offer Formula

```
[Desired Result] + [Specific Timeframe] + [Risk Reversal/Guarantee] + [Bonus] = Irresistible Offer

Example:
"Get 20 qualified sales meetings booked on your calendar in 30 days
 — or we work for free until you do. Plus: free CRM setup ($2,500 value)."
```

## Brunson's Epiphany Bridge Script

Use this 9-step structure for sales pages, webinars, and email stories:

```
1. The backstory: "I used to be just like you. I was struggling with [problem]..."
2. External desire: "I wanted [surface-level goal]..."
3. Internal desire: "But what I really wanted was [deeper emotional goal]..."
4. The wall: "I tried [thing 1], [thing 2], [thing 3]. Nothing worked."
5. The epiphany: "Then one day, I realized that [key insight]..."
6. The plan: "So I [what you did differently]..."
7. The result: "And within [timeframe], [specific measurable outcome]."
8. The transformation: "But the real change was [emotional/identity shift]."
9. The bridge: "That's why I created [product] — so you can skip the
              [years/mistakes] and go straight to [result]."
```

## Bullet Point Fascinations

Use these patterns for opt-in page bullets and sales page feature lists:

```
"How to [desired result] — even if [common objection]"
"The #1 mistake [dream buyer]s make with [topic] (and what to do instead)"
"Why [counterintuitive thing] actually [surprising result]"
"The [number]-minute trick that [specific measurable outcome]"
"What [authority figure] knows about [topic] that you don't"
"[Number] [things] you must never [do] (unless you want [bad outcome])"
```

## Guarantee / Risk Reversal Formulas

```
Basic: "30-day money-back guarantee. No questions asked."

Better: "Try it for 30 days. If you don't [specific result],
         I'll refund every penny AND let you keep everything."

Best: "Use [product] for 60 days. If you don't [measurable outcome],
       I'll refund your investment, let you keep the materials,
       AND give you $100 for wasting your time."
```

The stronger the guarantee, the higher the conversion — and paradoxically, the lower the refund rate (because it signals confidence).

## Subject Line Patterns (Email)

```
Curiosity: "I shouldn't be telling you this..."
Personal: "My barista made me realize something about [industry]"
Contrarian: "Stop [common advice everyone gives]"
Story: "The $50,000 mistake I almost made yesterday"
Social proof: "[Name] just [impressive result] with this"
Urgency: "Last chance: [specific thing] closes at midnight"
Question: "Are you making this [topic] mistake?"
```

## Price Anchoring Sequence

When presenting your offer on a sales page or webinar:

```
1. State the value of each component individually (high anchor)
2. Total them up: "That's $X,XXX in total value"
3. Reveal you're NOT charging that: "But I'm not going to charge you $X,XXX"
4. Lower anchor: "I'm not even going to charge you $X,XXX"
5. Reveal actual price: "Your investment today is just $XXX"
6. Reframe as daily cost: "That's less than [relatable daily expense] per day"
7. Compare to alternative: "That's less than [one hour of consulting / one bad hire / one month of the problem]"
```
