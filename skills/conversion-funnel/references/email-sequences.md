# Email Sequence Templates

## Sequence 1: Soap Opera Sequence (Brunson, Days 1–5)

For new subscribers. Build relationship through serialized storytelling.

### Day 1: "The Backstory"
```
Subject: [Something personal and curiosity-driven]

Set the stage. Who are you? What happened?
Share the pain you experienced — the same pain your reader has.
Be vulnerable. Be specific.

End with a cliffhanger: "But then everything changed when I discovered..."

No pitch. Just story.
```

### Day 2: "The Turning Point"
```
Subject: "So here's what happened next..."

Pick up exactly where you left off.
The drama. The wall you hit. The moment of despair.
Then: the epiphany moment. The insight that changed everything.

"I discovered that [key insight]..."

Soft mention of your approach (not product yet).
```

### Day 3: "The Epiphany"
```
Subject: "[The key insight from yesterday] changed everything"

Detail the solution you found.
Show the result — with specifics (numbers, timeframe).
First soft mention of your product/offer.

"I put everything I learned into [product name]..."

Light CTA: "If you're curious, here's more: [link]"
```

### Day 4: "The Hidden Benefits"
```
Subject: "I didn't expect THIS to happen"

Share unexpected benefits you or customers experienced.
Include 1-2 testimonials or case studies.
Stronger CTA to your offer.

"[Customer name] used this and [specific result]..."
```

### Day 5: "The Urgency"
```
Subject: "This is closing [tomorrow/Friday/soon]"

Deadline or scarcity element (must be real).
Recap the transformation: before → after.
Final push with risk reversal / guarantee.
Strong CTA.

"This is your last chance to [get result] at [this price]..."
```

## Sequence 2: Seinfeld Sequence (Day 6+, Ongoing)

Daily-ish emails. Entertaining. Always link back to your offer. "A show about nothing" — except every episode has a product plug.

### Format
```
Subject: [Curiosity-driven, personal, sometimes weird]

STORY (2-3 paragraphs):
Something that happened to you. Real, relatable, ideally funny or surprising.
Could be from today, from childhood, from a conversation.

LESSON (1 paragraph):
Connect the story to their problem or desire.
"And that's exactly what happens when [dream buyer]s try to [action] without [your solution]..."

CTA (1-2 sentences):
"Speaking of [lesson], here's how [product] helps: [link]"
```

### Example Subject Lines
```
"My barista made me realize something about [industry]"
"The $50,000 mistake I almost made yesterday"
"Why my 6-year-old is better at [skill] than most [role]s"
"I got yelled at for saying this at a conference"
"Netflix taught me everything about [topic]"
"The dumbest thing I did last week (and what it taught me)"
```

## Sequence 3: Godfather Sequence (Suby, for Unconverted Leads)

For leads who downloaded your lead magnet but haven't bought.

### Day 1: Pure Value
```
Subject: "Here's something I normally charge for"

Deliver genuine, actionable value. No pitch whatsoever.
A mini-framework, a checklist, an insight.
Build goodwill and reciprocity.
```

### Day 3: Case Study
```
Subject: "How [Name] went from [before] to [after]"

Tell a customer success story with specific numbers.
Focus on: their starting point → what they did → the result.
Soft CTA: "Want similar results? Here's how: [link]"
```

### Day 5: Objection Crusher
```
Subject: "The real reason you haven't [desired result] yet"

Address the #1 objection directly.
Usually one of: "I don't have time" / "It won't work for me" / "I can't afford it"
Reframe the objection: not doing it is MORE expensive.
```

### Day 7: The Godfather Offer
```
Subject: "I'm making you an offer you can't refuse"

Present an irresistible low-ticket or trial offer.
Stack the value. Add a bonus. Add a guarantee.
Make it a genuine no-brainer.
```

### Day 10: Last Chance
```
Subject: "Closing this at midnight"

Deadline reminder. Recap everything they get.
What they'll miss if they don't act.
Final CTA.
```

### Day 14: Breakup Email
```
Subject: "Should I stop emailing you?"

"I've sent you [X] emails about [topic].
You haven't responded, and I respect that.

If you're not interested, just click here and I'll remove you: [unsubscribe link]

But if you ARE still interested in [desired result],
hit reply and tell me what's holding you back.

Either way, I'm here."
```

This is counterintuitively one of the highest-converting emails. People respond to potential loss. Reply rates of 10-25% are common.

## Key Email Metrics

```
Open rate target:       25-40%
Click rate target:       3-7%
Unsubscribe rate:       Warning if > 1% per email
Reply rate (cold):       2-5% is good
Revenue per email sent:  Track this, optimize for this
```

## Email Deliverability Rules

- Warm up new domains (start with 50/day, increase 20% weekly)
- Always include plain text version
- Keep image-to-text ratio below 40:60
- Never use ALL CAPS in subject lines
- Include unsubscribe link (visible, not hidden)
- Clean list quarterly (remove non-openers after 90 days)
